Our Client
University of Wollongong Art Collection

The University of Wollongong Art Collection (UOWAC) has been a part of the University of Wollongong since the 1960s. Today the collection comprises over 5000 individual works ranging from paintings, prints, ceramics, textiles and a number of outdoor sculptures. Art works are displayed across several campus locations including UOW's main Wollongong campus, Innovation Campus, Nowra, Bateman's Bay, Bega, Moss Vale, Loftus and the Sydney Business School. UOWAC aims to collect and care for works of art with significance to the University and wider Illawarra region. Art is an integral part of campus life for students, staff, the local community and wider art lovers who want to enjoy the collection.

Why This App?
Easy to use
 
User-friendly interface with integrated map and navigation to help users enjoy the artworks with no difficulties.
Real-time push notification
To improve the user experience, visitors will receive real-time push notifications when they visit outdoor sculptures.
Social engagement
 
Users can easily sign in via social login (Google and Facebook), join others in enjoying outdoor sculptures with likes and comments.
Multi-platform support
The application is built for both iOS and Android to support as many users as possible.

Design Methodology: Kanban
What is Kanban ?
Kanban is a popular methodology used for the implementation of agile software development.
This framework can help increase the team’s productivity by focusing on real-time communication, continual delivery and not overburdening the development team.
Kanban Basic Principles
Visualize the workflow
Limit work in progress
Continuous enhancement
Focus on flow
Kanban Benefits
Flexible planning
Shorter time cycles
Fewer bottlenecks
Quick responsive to change
Visual metrics
Continuous delivery

ABOUT NUCLEUS
Our Mission
